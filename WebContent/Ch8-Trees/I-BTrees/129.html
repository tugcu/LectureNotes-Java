Performance of Our B-Tree

  • With M=342, the height of our tree for N students will be \(\lceil log_{342} \lceil \tfrac N L \rceil \rceil\)
  • For example, with N = 100,000 (about 10 times the size of student population at Boğaziçi University) the height of the tree with M=342 would be no more than 2, because \(\lceil log_{342} (25,000) \rceil = 2\)
  • So, any student record can be found in 3 disk accesses. If the root of the B-Tree is stored in memory, then only 2 disk accesses are needed
